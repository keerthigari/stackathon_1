Insomnia

Insomnia is a sleeping disorder you have trouble falling and /or staying asleep. The condition can be short-term (acute) or can last a long time (chronic). It may also come and go. Acute insomnia lasts from 1 night to a few weeks. Insomnia is chronic when it happens at least 3 nights a week for 3 months or more.

Types of Insomnia:

1.Primary insomnia: This means your sleep problems aren’t linked to any other health condition or problem.

2.Secondary insomnia:This means you have trouble sleeping because of a health condition (like asthma, depression, arthritis, cancer, or heartburn); pain; medication; or substance use (like alcohol).

3.Sleep-onset insomnia: This means you have trouble getting to sleep.

4.Sleep-maintenance insomnia: This happens when you have trouble staying asleep through the night or wake up too early.

5.Mixed insomnia: With this type of insomnia, you have trouble both falling asleep and staying asleep through the night.

6.Paradoxical insomnia: When you have paradoxical insomnia, you underestimate the time you're asleep. It feels like you sleep a lot less than you really do.

SYMPTOMS
Symptoms of insomnia include:

1.Sleepiness during the day
2.Fatigue
3.Grumpiness
4.Problems with concentration or memory
5.Difficulty falling asleep at night
6.Waking up during the night
7.Waking up too early
8.Not feeling well-rested after a night's sleep
9.Daytime tiredness or sleepiness
10.Irritability, depression or anxiety
11.Difficulty paying attention, focusing on tasks or remembering
12.Increased errors or accidents
13.Ongoing worries about sleep
